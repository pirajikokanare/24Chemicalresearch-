<strong>Definition</strong></p><p>
</p><p><strong>Para Amino Phenol (PAP)</strong>, chemically known as <strong>C6H7NO</strong>, is an aromatic amine and a vital chemical compound in various industrial applications. PAP is primarily used in the production of hair dye intermediates, primarily in the form of <strong>para-phenylenediamine</strong> (PPD), which is a key component of permanent hair dyes. It is also a significant precursor in the synthesis of <strong>pharmaceutical products</strong> and <strong>specialty chemicals</strong> used in the manufacture of dyes, and as a stabilizer in photographic chemicals.</p><p>
</p><p>Para Amino Phenol is a colorless to pale yellow crystalline compound that is soluble in water and alcohol. It is mainly derived from <strong>coal tar</strong> or through synthetic processes in chemical manufacturing plants. While its most notable application remains in hair coloring products, its versatility spans various industries such as <strong>pharmaceuticals</strong>, <strong>photography</strong>, and <strong>polymer production</strong>.</p><p>
