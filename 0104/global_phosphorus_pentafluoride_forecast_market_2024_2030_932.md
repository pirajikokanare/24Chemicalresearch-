Phosphorus pentafluoride (chemical formula: PF5) is a phosphorus halogen compound, and the phosphorus atom has an oxidation number of +5 and contains a three-center four-electron bond. Phosphorus pentafluoride is a colorless malodorous gas under normal temperature and pressure, and it is highly irritating to skin, eyes and mucous membranes. It is a highly reactive compound that is strongly hydrolyzed in humid air to produce toxic and corrosive white hydrogen fumes. Phosphorus pentafluoride is commonly used to generate gases and as a polymerization catalyst.</p><p>
