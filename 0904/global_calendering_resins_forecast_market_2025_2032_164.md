</p><p><strong>Definition</strong></p><p>
</p><p>Calendering resins refer to a class of polymeric materials used in the calendering process, a technique primarily used for producing thin films, sheets, and coated fabrics. These resins, including polyvinyl chloride (PVC), polyethylene (PE), and polypropylene (PP), provide excellent durability, flexibility, and heat resistance. Calendering resins are widely used across industries such as packaging, automotive, construction, and textiles due to their ability to produce high-quality, uniform materials with precise thickness control.</p><p>
