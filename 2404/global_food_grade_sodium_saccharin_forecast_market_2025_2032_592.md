<strong>Definition</strong></p><p>
</p><p>Food grade sodium saccharin is a synthetic sweetener that is approximately 300-400 times sweeter than sucrose, or table sugar. It is a white, odorless, crystalline powder that is soluble in water. Sodium saccharin is approved by the FDA for use as a food additive in the United States.</p><p>
