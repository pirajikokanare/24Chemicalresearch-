<strong>Definition</strong></p><p>
</p><p>Glass and rock wool insulation are widely used thermal insulation materials that help improve energy efficiency in residential, commercial, and industrial applications. Glass wool is manufactured from molten glass spun into fine fibers, while rock wool is produced from volcanic rock processed in a similar manner. Both materials provide excellent heat resistance, soundproofing, and fire resistance, making them integral to sustainable construction practices.</p><p>
