<strong>Definition</strong></p><p>
</p><p>Fire-resistant flexible sealants are specialized materials used to seal openings in buildings that need to meet fire safety regulations. These sealants are typically used in joints, cracks, and gaps in fire-rated walls, floors, and ceilings. They are designed to maintain their integrity when exposed to high temperatures, preventing the spread of fire and smoke, thus protecting the structural elements of buildings and the occupants inside.</p><p>
</p><p>These sealants are manufactured from a variety of materials, including silicone, intumescent materials, and acrylics, each providing different degrees of flexibility, fire resistance, and durability. When exposed to high temperatures, many of these sealants undergo a chemical reaction, expanding to fill gaps and block the passage of fire and smoke.</p><p>
</p><p>The use of fire-resistant flexible sealants is mandated in building codes for certain areas within residential, commercial, and industrial construction projects. Their primary function is to maintain the integrity of fire-rated assemblies, thus playing a vital role in fire prevention and safety strategies.</p><p>
