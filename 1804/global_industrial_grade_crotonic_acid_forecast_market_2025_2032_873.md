Definition</p><p>
</p><p><strong>Industrial Grade Crotonic Acid</strong> refers to crotonic acid produced and processed specifically to meet industrial requirements. It is used in various applications, primarily in the chemical industry. Crotonic acid is a colorless liquid with a pungent odor, and it features a conjugated double bond structure, which makes it highly reactive and useful in different industrial processes.</p><p>
Key Features:</p><p>
</p><p><strong>Chemical Formula</strong>: C4H6O2</p><p><strong>Molecular Structure</strong>: It contains a conjugated system (C=C) that makes it reactive and suitable for polymerization.</p><p><strong>Physical Properties</strong>: It is a liquid that is soluble in water, alcohols, and ether.</p><p><strong>Production</strong>: It is typically synthesized via the aldol condensation of acetic acid or through other chemical methods.</p><p>
</p><p>Crotonic acid is used in the manufacture of numerous chemical products, including plastics, coatings, adhesives, surfactants, and agrochemicals. Its ability to undergo polymerization and crosslinking makes it valuable in industries requiring materials with enhanced durability and performance.</p><p>
