</p><p>Ethylidenecyclohexane (CAS 1003-64-1) is an organic compound belonging to the class of substituted cycloalkenes. Structurally, it consists of a cyclohexane ring substituted by an ethylidene group, resulting in a compound that is both chemically reactive and industrially versatile. Ethylidenecyclohexane is particularly known for its reactivity in oxidation reactions and its role as a substrate in Lewis-acid catalyzed transformations, notably with azodicarboxylates and various alkenes. Its unique structure makes it valuable in synthetic organic chemistry, and it is increasingly used in industrial applications such as pharmaceuticals, agrochemicals, and specialty chemicals.</p><p>
</p><p>One of the compound's key industrial applications includes its oxidation via purified ethylbenzene dehydrogenase, which underscores its biochemical utility. This oxidation pathway is significant in biocatalysis and green chemistry domains, as it promotes more sustainable synthetic methodologies. Moreover, its effectiveness in catalyzed azodicarboxylate reactions adds to its growing demand in advanced material synthesis and polymer research.</p><p>
