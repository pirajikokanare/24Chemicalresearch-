<strong>Definition</strong></p><p>
</p><p>Ethylene Propylene Diene Monomer (EPDM) is a type of synthetic rubber that is highly valued for its durability and versatile properties. Made from a combination of ethylene, propylene, and a diene monomer, EPDM possesses excellent resistance to heat, oxidation, ozone, weathering, and a variety of chemicals. These properties make EPDM an ideal material for a wide range of applications, including automotive parts, electrical insulation, roofing, and gaskets.</p><p>
</p><p>EPDM's key characteristics include its ability to withstand extreme temperatures, both high and low, making it ideal for applications that experience temperature fluctuations. Additionally, it is known for its flexibility, abrasion resistance, and longevity, which is why it is commonly used in industrial and commercial applications. It can also be finished to a smooth surface, enhancing its applicability in sealing and gasketing solutions.</p><p>
</p><p>The global market for EPDM is driven by the increasing demand for high-performance materials in the automotive, construction, and electrical industries. The compound is used to manufacture various products such as seals, hoses, belts, and roofing membranes. As industries seek more durable, weather-resistant, and cost-effective materials, EPDM continues to gain traction in several key markets worldwide.</p><p>
