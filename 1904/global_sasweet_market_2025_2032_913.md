Overview</p><p>
</p><p>Sandasweet (Sandalore), also known by its chemical name CAS 65113-99-7, is a colorless to pale yellow liquid with the molecular formula C14H26O. It is a synthetic fragrance compound primarily used in the production of wood-flavored scents in daily chemical products such as perfumes, air fresheners, and other consumer goods. This compound has a subtle, sandalwood-like aroma that is highly valued for its ability to enhance the sensory experience of a product, imparting a warm, rich, and earthy fragrance. Although Sandasweet (Sandalore) is widely used in non-edible flavors, it remains relatively uncommon in edible flavor formulations due to regulatory restrictions and consumer preferences.</p><p>
</p><p>Sandasweet is valued not only for its pleasant scent but also for its stability and ability to mimic the complexity of natural sandalwood. Given its versatility, Sandasweet has found its way into various industries, including personal care, cosmetics, and home products. Its use as a substitute for real sandalwood, which can be scarce and expensive, makes it a highly sought-after alternative in the fragrance industry.</p><p>
