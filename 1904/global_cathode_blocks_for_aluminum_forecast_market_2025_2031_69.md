</p><p>Cathode blocks for aluminum, also known as cathode carbon blocks, are essential components used in the electrolysis process of aluminum production. Positioned at the bottom of electrolytic cells, they serve as the cathode in the electrochemical setup, working in conjunction with the anode to form the electrolytic system. These blocks are primarily composed of carbon and are designed to conduct electricity efficiently while enduring the harsh operating conditions inside the electrolytic cells, including exposure to molten aluminum, electrolyte salts, and reactive gases. Their performance directly influences the efficiency, lifespan, and energy consumption of the aluminum smelting process. As such, they play a critical role in the operational success of aluminum electrolysis cells.</p><p>
