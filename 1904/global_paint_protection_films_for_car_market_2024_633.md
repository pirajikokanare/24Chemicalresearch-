<h1>Global Paint Protection Films for Car Market Research Report 2024(Status and Outlook)</h1><p>Paint Protection Film (PPF), also known as clear bra, clear paint film, or simply clear film, is a thermoplastic urethane film that provides protective coverage for automotive exteriors. It is primarily applied to exposed painted areas of vehicles, such as side mirrors, hoods, door edges, door handle cavities, bumpers, rocker panels, and wheel flares. The film is engineered to guard against a variety of hazards including stone chips, bug damage, automotive fluid stains, road tar stains, scratches, and harsh outdoor weathering conditions. PPF is typically made from a blend of polymers, including polymeric calendered PVC film, adhesives, and clay-coated paper. The film is transparent, maintaining the vehicle's aesthetic while offering robust protection from physical damage.</p><p>
</p><p>PPF’s key purpose is to preserve the appearance and integrity of a vehicle’s paint job, extending the vehicle's lifespan and maintaining its resale value.
