</p><p>Pleated Membrane Filtration refers to a filtration process that utilizes pleated polymer membrane filters, which are engineered to remove particles and microorganisms from fluids. These filters are typically made of materials such as polyethersulfone (PES), polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE), nylon, and polypropylene. They are constructed with a high surface area due to the pleated design, which enhances the filtration capacity and lifespan compared to flat membrane filters. The membranes come in specific pore sizes, usually ranging from 0.1 to 5 microns, and function by capturing contaminants on the surface or within the depth of the membrane structure.</p><p>
</p><p>Pleated membrane filters are widely used across industries including pharmaceuticals, food and beverage, biotechnology, microelectronics, water treatment, and laboratories. Their key advantage lies in high flow rates, chemical resistance, and reliable retention of particulates and microorganisms, making them indispensable in critical filtration applications.</p><p>
