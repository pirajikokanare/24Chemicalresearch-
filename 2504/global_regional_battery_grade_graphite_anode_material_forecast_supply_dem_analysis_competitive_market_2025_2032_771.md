</p><p>Battery Grade Graphite Anode Material refers to high-purity graphite materials specifically engineered for use in lithium-ion batteries. These materials form the anode, a critical component in battery technology, responsible for storing and releasing electrical energy. The quality and characteristics of graphite anode materials significantly impact the battery's performance, longevity, and efficiency. Natural and synthetic graphite are the two main types used in battery applications, with advancements in technology driving the development of high-performance variants to meet the growing demand in industries such as electric vehicles (EVs), consumer electronics, and energy storage systems.</p><p>
