</p><p>Behenyl alcohol, also known as docosanol, is a 22-carbon long-chain fatty alcohol derived primarily from natural vegetable sources such as coconut oil and rapeseed oil. It exists as a white, waxy solid at room temperature and is known for its emulsifying, thickening, and stabilizing properties. Its chemical structure enables it to function effectively in both water and oil phases, making it an essential ingredient in a wide range of formulations.</p><p>
</p><p>Commonly used in the personal care and cosmetics industry, behenyl alcohol acts as an opacifying agent, emollient, and viscosity-increasing agent. It is also employed in pharmaceutical applications, most notably in antiviral treatments such as cold sore creams containing docosanol. Beyond healthcare and cosmetics, behenyl alcohol finds use in industrial lubricants, plastic additives, and surfactants due to its hydrophobic properties.</p><p>
</p><p>With growing global trends favoring plant-based, biodegradable ingredients, behenyl alcohol is increasingly recognized as a sustainable alternative to synthetic thickeners and stabilizers. Its versatility, combined with its environmental friendliness, positions it as a critical component in modern formulations across several industries.</p><p>
