<h1>Global Hydrotalcite (CAS 11097-59-9) Market Research Report 2025-2032</h1><p><strong>Hydrotalcite</strong>, a type of Layered Double Hydroxide (LDH), is an inorganic compound known for its distinctive structure and high water content. Its name originates from its resemblance to talc, a soft mineral, and its ability to retain significant amounts of water. This material is characterized by positively charged hydroxide layers interspersed with anionic species, giving it remarkable anion-exchange properties. The compound’s primary form, hydrotalcite, is sourced from nature in limited quantities, found in places such as Snarum in Norway and the Ural Mountains in Russia.</p><p>
</p><p>In industrial applications, hydrotalcite serves multiple roles due to its ability to trap and exchange ions. This capability makes it a vital ingredient in various sectors, including plastics, pharmaceuticals, and medical applications.
